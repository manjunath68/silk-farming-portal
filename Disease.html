Diseases of Silkworm and Control Measures

I. Fungal Diseases of Silkworm

a. Muscardine

Causative Agent:

  • White Muscardine - Beauveria bassiana
  • Green Muscardine - Metarrhizium anisopliae

Symptoms: Black spots, loss of appetite, mummification with white/green fungal growth.

b. Aspergillosis

Causative Agent: Aspergillus flavus, Aspergillus oryzae

Symptoms: Larvae become inactive, develop brownish-green color after death.

II. Viral Diseases of Silkworm

a. Grasserie (Nuclear Polyhedrosis)

Causative Agent: B. mori Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus (BmNPV)

Symptoms: Shining, fragile skin, swollen intersegmental regions, milky haemolymph.

b. Kenchu

Causative Agent: Densonucleosis Virus (DNV)

Symptoms: Flaccid body, growth retardation, reddish translucent appearance.

III. Microsporidian Disease - Pebrine

Causative Agent: Nosema bombycis

Symptoms: Clumped eggs, curled moth wings, irregular larval growth.

IV. Bacterial Diseases - Bacterial Flacherie

Causative Agent: Streptococcus, Staphylococcus, Serratia marscescens, Bacillus thuringiensis

Symptoms: Soft, flaccid larvae, foul-smelling decomposition.

General Prevention and Control Measures

  • Disinfection of rearing house, appliances, and surroundings.
  • Bed disinfection from early stages.
  • Regulation of temperature, humidity, and proper ventilation.
  • Removal and disposal of infected larvae.
  • Providing high-quality mulberry leaves.
