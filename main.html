Why Silk Farming?

  • Silk farming, also known as sericulture, is an ancient practice that involves the rearing of silkworms to produce silk.
  • India is the second-largest silk producer in the world, with Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, and West Bengal being the top silk-producing states.
  • The demand for organic and sustainable silk is growing, making sericulture a profitable and eco-friendly business..

History of Silk Farming

  • Silk farming originated in China around 2700 BC and remained a secret for centuries.
  • The Silk Route played a major role in spreading silk production techniques across the world.
  • India has a rich silk heritage, producing four main types of silk:-
    • Mulberry Silk (most common, ~70% of India's production)
    • Tasar Silk (produced by wild silkworms)
    • Eri Silk (soft, warm, and durable)
    • Muga Silk (exclusive to Assam, known for its golden shine)
